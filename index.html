Simple

Does one job well

It's not a game engine - vrEmbed is optimized to just take your 2D and 3D images and give you versatile options to display them in VR.

No apps

Use anywhere

No need for a native app - works in mobile and desktop browsers, and loads fast - the Javascript is less than 150kb

Easy

Build embeds fast

Simple embed codes to create a vrEmbed from any image - these can be chained together to make entire VR stories!

Accessible

Stereo blindness

At least 10% of the population can't use VR headsets or see 3D - vrEmbed won't leave these users behind.

Free

Open Source

vrEmbed is open source!

Compatible

Headset support

vrEmbed has a support for an ever-growing range of viewing modes, from red-blue glasses to Google Cardboard and beyond.

One-line embed example

<a class="vrEmbedPhoto" src="src/assets/embarcadero_center.jpg" isStereo="false" sphereParams="360,180,0,0"></a><script async src="//lisa-wolfgang.github.io/vrEmbed/vrEmbed.min.js" charset="utf-8"></script>




Who is vrEmbed for?

  • Artists and storytellers who want to experiment with narratives in VR.
  • Photographers who want to add a sense of immersion to their existing photos.
  • Folks with 3D-stereo images who need an up-to-date medium to showcase their work.
  • Academics and researchers who want an easy, extensible platform to experiment with storytelling and interaction techniques in VR.

Open-source goodness

vrEmbed is build using the tiny-but-fast twgl and browserify. These pages are laid out using skeleton.
